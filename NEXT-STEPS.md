## Decision Points

### Should You Bootstrap or Fundraise?

**Bootstrap if**:
- You can build MVP yourself or with co-founders
- You have savings or part-time income
- You want to move methodically
- You prefer full control

**Fundraise if**:
- You need to hire team quickly
- Market timing is critical
- Competition requires speed
- You want to scale fast

### Should You Go Full-Time Now?

**Go full-time if**:
- You have 6-12 months runway
- Co-founder(s) are committed
- Strong customer validation
- Can't build while working

**Stay part-time if**:
- Still validating idea
- Need to build MVP first
- Financial constraints
- Can make progress nights/weekends

## Questions to Answer

Before proceeding, ensure you can answer:

1. **Problem**: What specific problem are you solving?
2. **Customer**: Who is your ideal first customer?
3. **Solution**: Why is your solution better than alternatives?
4. **Market**: How big is the opportunity?
5. **Timing**: Why now?
6. **Team**: Do you have the right team/skills?
7. **Traction**: What validation do you have?
8. **Business Model**: How will you make money?
9. **Go-to-Market**: How will you acquire customers?
10. **Competitive Advantage**: What's your moat?

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Stop or pivot if**:
- No one will pay for it (after 20+ conversations)
- Customer acquisition cost > lifetime value
- Team isn't working well together
- Market is too small
- Can't differentiate from competitors
- Consistently missing milestones by 2x+

---

## Success Signals

**You're on the right track if**:
- Customers ask when they can use it
- People willing to pay before it's built
- Organic word-of-mouth starting
- Users coming back regularly
- Strong engagement metrics
- Competitors taking notice
