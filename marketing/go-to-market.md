# ObjectaLabs Go-to-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

ObjectaLabs's go-to-market strategy focuses on a bottom-up, product-led growth approach initially, evolving to enterprise sales as we scale. We'll leverage content marketing, community building, and strategic partnerships to establish market presence.

---

## Market Entry Strategy

### Phase 1: Product-Led Growth (Months 1-6)
**Target**: Early adopters, developers, small businesses

**Approach**: Free tier + viral features

### Phase 2: Sales-Assisted Growth (Months 7-12)
**Target**: Mid-market companies (50-500 employees)

**Approach**: Inside sales + PLG

### Phase 3: Enterprise Sales (Year 2+)
**Target**: Fortune 500, large enterprises

**Approach**: Field sales + account-based marketing

## Sales Strategy

### Phase 1: Self-Service (Months 1-6)
**No sales team**: 100% product-led

**Conversion Funnel**:
1. Sign up (free tier)
2. Activation (create first agent)
3. Value delivery (100+ interactions)
4. Upgrade prompt (hit limits)
5. Paid conversion

**Optimization**:
- Onboarding flow
- In-app messaging
- Email nurture sequences
- Product improvements

### Phase 2: Sales-Assisted (Months 7-12)
**Team**: 2 inside sales reps

**Focus**: Inbound leads from marketing

**Process**:
1. Lead scoring (free trial users + engagement)
2. Outreach (email + calls)
3. Demo/consultation
4. Trial extension (if needed)
5. Close

**Quota**: $40K/month per rep

### Phase 3: Enterprise Sales (Year 2+)
**Team**: 4 account executives + 2 SEs

**Focus**: Outbound + ABM

**Process**:
1. Account research
2. Multi-threaded outreach
3. Discovery call
4. Technical evaluation (POC)
5. Security review
6. Legal/procurement
7. Close

**Average Deal Size**: $100K/year
**Sales Cycle**: 6-9 months

## Customer Acquisition

### Free Trial Strategy

**Freemium Model**:
- Always free tier (limited)
- Credit card NOT required
- Upgrade prompts at limits

**Trial Benefits**:
- Low friction signup
- Viral potential
- Product validation
- Data collection

**Conversion Tactics**:
1. Usage-based triggers
   - Hit message limit
   - Need more agents
   - Want integrations

2. Time-based emails
   - Day 1: Welcome + tutorial
   - Day 3: Tips for success
   - Day 7: Use cases inspiration
   - Day 14: Upgrade benefits
   - Day 21: Special offer (20% off)
   - Day 28: Last chance

3. In-app messaging
   - Feature callouts

### Referral Program

## Competitive Response

### If Big Tech Launches Competing Product

**Strategy**:
1. **Double down on differentiation**
   - Better UX/ease of use
   - Multi-LLM flexibility
   - Specialized features

2. **Move faster**
   - Rapid iteration
   - Customer feedback loops
   - New features monthly

3. **Go vertical**
   - Industry-specific solutions
   - Deep integrations
   - Domain expertise

4. **Build community**
   - Loyal user base
   - Strong brand
   - Switching costs

### If New Startup Emerges

**Strategy**:
1. **Monitor closely**
   - Track their features
   - Understand positioning
   - Identify gaps

2. **Maintain lead**
   - Accelerate roadmap
   - Lock in customers
   - Build moats (data, integrations)

3. **Partner or acquire**
   - Complementary? Partner
   - Threatening? Consider acquisition
