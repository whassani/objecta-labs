## Presentation Tips

### Before the Meeting
- [ ] Research investors (portfolio, thesis, interests)
- [ ] Prepare custom version (highlight relevant angles)
- [ ] Practice pitch (aim for 15 minutes + 15 Q&A)
- [ ] Test tech (screen sharing, video, etc.)
- [ ] Send deck 24 hours before (optional)

### During the Pitch
- Start with a story (hook them)
- Maintain eye contact (don't read slides)
- Show passion and conviction
- Be concise (investors will ask questions)
- Handle questions confidently
- Ask for the money (clear ask)

### After the Meeting
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Share any requested materials
- Provide updates on progress
- Follow up on timeline

### Common Questions to Prepare For
1. Why now? (market timing)
2. Why you? (team credibility)
3. What's your moat? (defensibility)
4. How do you acquire customers? (GTM)
5. What about [big competitor]? (competition)
6. What are the risks? (challenges)
7. How much are you raising? (fundraising)
8. What's the exit opportunity? (returns)
9. What keeps you up at night? (concerns)
10. Why should we invest? (why us)

## Design Guidelines

### Visual Style
- Clean and professional
- Consistent color scheme (brand colors)
- High-quality images/screenshots
- Minimal text (use bullet points)
- Clear hierarchy

### Font
- Sans-serif (modern)
- Max 2 font families
- Large enough to read (min 24pt)

### Layout
- One main idea per slide
- Plenty of white space
- Consistent header placement
- Branded footer

### Data Visualization
- Simple charts (bar, line, pie)
- Clear labels
- Use color meaningfully
- Source citations
